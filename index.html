My name is Daniel and I am here to participate in the conception and creation of alternatives to the current digital paradigm.

Vewpoint is another proposal although its proposal does not intend to be like the others.

The present technological context is rapidly transforming the way we communicate and coordinate as a species, allowing us to bypass all kinds of physical, geographical or political conditions.

We are part of a global network, mediated through devices that model our behavior, at the same time that our actions and reactions shape the tools that constitute the network itself.

Technology and society are intertwined in a fabric of compromises and dependencies that makes it impossible to understand them separately. They are both cause and effect, one of the other.

I am here because of my frustration with the current state of affairs, after understanding that there are alternatives and that change is possible. After understanding that we have to explore those alternatives because change is required.

I am here because I firmly believe in human potential, in our ability to recalibrate, to find balance.

Because behind all the complexity in the systems that support the contemporary lifestyle, there are people, like you and me, making decisions based on their conception of the world.

Because every perspective is relevant in the conversation that establishes the values upon which complexity emerges and impacts our lives, the values that define us as a society.

Our shared reality depends on the constant interaction between all the private realities that constitute it.

We live in the Information Age, and the most advanced technology available to us right now is the Internet, with the Web being its most popular application. I've decided to continue in this direction, and to me it only makes sense if I do it in an open and collaborative way, something that is not possible without first introducing myself.

This is what I'm doing.

As I take off the VR headset to take a break, I find myself surrounded by a group of people, most of them sitting on the floor, waiting. After a few moments, they give me a round of applause, and as they leave, I realize that they have left a note behind.

I feel touched every time I remember the moment I read it.

The same day, again when I take off the VR headset, I find a couple of coins inside my rectangle. I use the break to go to the museum bar and transform the coins into a heart-shaped chocolate pastry.

Go back to the note.
